ABOC Mastercard

Role
  • UI Designer
  • Front-end Dev
Timeframe
  • 3 weeks
Industry
  • Personal credit card
  • Finance

Introduction

The ABOC Platinum Rewards Mastercard helps consumers rebuild credit while earning everyday rewards. I designed and coded a landing page that communicates benefits clearly, builds trust quickly, and moves qualified users toward application without friction.

01The Challenge

Compete in a crowded credit market and overcome skepticism from users who expect confusing terms. The page needed to present a credible offer, keep attention on the path to apply, and make key benefits unmistakable.

Core value points included $0 annual fee, credit lines up to $2,500, and monthly reporting to credit bureaus. The challenge was to surface these early, explain them plainly, and avoid cognitive overload.

02Design Approach

The visual language is simple and trustworthy. I used a strong dark blue base for stability with light blue and red accents for emphasis and state changes. Layout modules are scannable, copy is concise, and the information hierarchy leads with the primary benefit.

Accessibility and mobile performance were priorities. Type sizes, contrast, and spacing support readability on small screens, while clear headings and short paragraphs improve scan speed.

03The Process

1. Hero with immediate value

The hero presents the promise to rebuild credit and earn rewards, followed by a short list of proof points users care about most.

  • $0 annual fee
  • Credit lines up to $2,500
  • Monthly reporting to credit bureaus

Dual CTAs let users either apply or learn more. The path is clear for both ready and curious visitors.

2. Benefit details without noise

Supporting sections expand on features using plain language and simple iconography. The goal is clarity, not hype.

3. Product credibility

  • Prominent card imagery (front and back) anchors the brand and reinforces legitimacy at a glance.

4. Consistent, respectful CTAs

  • Buttons use the light blue accent for visibility, maintain consistent placement, and avoid aggressive patterns. Users always know what to do next, without pressure.

04Final Outcome

The page delivers a clear message, faster comprehension, and a smoother route to apply. Post-launch metrics showed stronger engagement and intent.

  • Engagement increased 17 percent, with more users exploring details and product imagery.
  • Conversions increased 28 percent, driven by clearer hierarchy and predictable CTAs.
  • Bounce rate decreased 13 percent, indicating better information scent and trust.

The final experience balances clarity and brand presence, performs well on mobile, and supports users who want to evaluate before committing.
